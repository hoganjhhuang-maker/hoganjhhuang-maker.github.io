About Me

I am a second-year MA student in Translation Studies at the Graduate Institute of Interpretation and Translation (GIIT), Shanghai International Studies University, under the supervision of Associate Professor Zhang Ying. I expect to graduate in June 2027.

My research focus lies in the history of translation. Specifically, my earliest interest was the history of foreign translations of Chinese opera. I am curious about the characteristics presented by Yuan Opera (Yuan Zaju)—the earliest traditional Chinese opera introduced to the English-speaking world—across different historical periods, as well as the reasons behind these characteristics. I seek to understand how these features relate to the development and evolution of society, politics, and poetics behind different historical stages. As you might notice, this is deeply influenced by the cultural turn in translation studies, which may be attributed to the academic training I received in GIIT. Following the lineage starting from Professor Xie Tianzhen, our approach has gradually liberated translation studies from purely prescriptive research, turning instead toward the description of texts and the interpretation of the social, historical, and cultural contexts of the target language country. This shift, of course, is also informed by the international development of translation studies.

With the intense impact of the digital and AI era, the humanities have been significantly affected. Coincidentally, this began just as I entered my graduate studies, which means my research career was destined from the start to be closely integrated with digital technology and artificial intelligence. While traditional humanistic methods remain important, the significance of new empirical research paradigms in this era cannot be ignored. Since the 1990s, translation studies have utilized corpus technology for qualitative research. However, it must be admitted that until recently, Social Network Analysis (SNA) has not been widely used in translation studies, especially in Mainland China. Based on the view of translation history as a collection of all social relations related to translation, I believe that SNA is highly effective for historical research. Therefore, I have studied the application of SNA in the fields of literature and history and migrated these methods into translation research, an area I will continue to explore. I believe that the use of SNA can reveal macro-phenomena that traditional humanistic research and simple close reading cannot discover—what Franco Moretti calls "Distant Reading." Furthermore, acknowledging the increasing influence of digital technology on humanistic research, I am also exploring the integration of Python-based text mining and R-powered data analysis and network modeling in my research, especially the capacity of network modeling to explain the formation and features of relational networks.

Those interested in translation history often maintain a broader interest in history. My interest in political history has led me to follow developments in historiography alongside my translation research, particularly the history of the 1900 Loyalist Uprising and the Baohuang Hui (Chinese Empire Reform Association) in the late Qing and early Republic periods. As a researcher of translation history, my focus naturally falls on the events within the Baohuang Hui related to translation. Initially, my interest centered on the translators surrounding the key figure Kang Youwei; my original question was simple: who were these translators? As my research deepened, beyond merely identifying their names, I became more concerned with their identities, political and ideological stances. Researching the history of translation within the Baohuang Hui is a direction I also hope to pursue in the future. I believe clarifying these issues will not only benefit translation history by revealing the vital role of translation in a major historical event like the Baohuang Hui, but also contribute to historiography. From the perspective of translation, we may be able to see a different version of history.

Research Interests
